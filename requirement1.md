# Health Treatment and Provider Matching platform

# 1. Executive Summary

This business is a med-tech distribution and orchestration platform, not a
healthcare provider.

It acts as a single, free entry point into modern, non-surgical health treatments by
delivering an advanced, data-driven consultation experience that spans the full
treatment landscape.

Users complete:

- One health intake
- When appropriate, one blood test

They receive:

- A structured health overview
- Evidence-based treatment pathways
- Matched provider options

The platform:

- Does not diagnose
- Does not prescribe
- Does not deliver care

All medical responsibility sits with licensed third-party providers.

For consumers:

- Access is free
- Recommendations are comprehensive
- Costs are significantly lower than private clinics

The platform is monetised entirely on the supply side, through performance-based
relationships with providers.

—

# 2. Problem

Modern health treatments exist in a fragmented, inefficient ecosystem.

Today:

- Consumers must self-educate across dozens of providers
- Each provider offers a narrow, biased view of solutions
- Consultations are expensive and repetitive
- Blood tests are duplicated unnecessarily


There is no single place where a consumer can:

- Share health context once
- Understand which treatments are appropriate
- Compare providers on true clinical fit
- Enter treatment without paying upfront consultation fees

This problem is unsolved at mass-market scale.

—

# 3. Solution

A centralised, AI-driven platform that functions as a free, expert-level health
treatment navigator.

The platform:

- Collects structured health and preference data
- Determines diagnostic requirements
- Analyses blood results
- Identifies appropriate treatment pathways
- Routes users to compatible providers

From the user’s perspective, it feels like:

A highly experienced clinician who understands the entire treatment market and is
not limited to a single clinic or product set.

The consultation experience is free.

Providers, not users, fund the system.

—

# 4. User Journey

# Step 1: Health Intake & Preferences

Users complete a modular questionnaire covering:

- Medical and family history
- Symptoms and goals
- Risk tolerance
- Treatment openness
- Delivery preferences
- Diet and lifestyle


- Budget sensitivity
- Subscription preferences

Skipping is allowed, but accuracy improves with detail.

# Step 2: Diagnostic Decision Engine

The platform determines:

- Whether blood testing is required
- Which biomarkers are relevant
- The minimum viable diagnostic panel

Users may select:

- Targeted panels
- Goal-based panels
- Comprehensive panels

# Step 3: Blood Testing

- Completed via partner labs
- Results flow directly into the platform
- Structured for provider consumption
-
Blood testing is priced competitively and may be:
- Break-even
- Or subsidised as a loss leader

Aside from testing, the platform remains free.

# Step 4: AI-Driven Analysis & Education

The platform generates:

- A clear health summary
- Explained biomarker insights
- Appropriate treatment pathways
- Compatible provider options
- Suggested supplement strategies

All outputs are:

- Educational
- Non-diagnostic
- Designed to support informed decision-making

# Step 5: Provider Hando ff


Users select:

- A treatment pathway
- A provider

The platform transfers:

- Intake data
- Blood results
- Eligibility context

Providers:

- Confirm medical suitability
- Prescribe if applicable
- Deliver care
- Manage follow-ups and subscriptions

—

# 5. Platform Boundaries

The platform:

- Aggregates and analyses data
- Educates users
- Pre-screens eligibility
- Enables discovery and routing

The platform does not:

- Diagnose
- Prescribe
- Override clinicians
- Deliver medical care

# 14. Data Collection, Intelligence Layer & Monetisation

# 1. Nature of Data Collected

The platform collects high-value, structured health intelligence, not raw medical
records.

Primary data categories:

- Longitudinal biomarker data (blood tests over time)
- Structured symptom and goal data
- Treatment pathways selected
- Provider outcomes (initiation, continuation, churn)
- Anonymised demographic and lifestyle attributes
- Price sensitivity and subscription behaviour
- Treatment efficacy signals (proxy outcomes, not diagnoses)

All data is:

- User-consented
- Stored securely
- Separated into identifiable and anonymised layers
- Governed under GDPR (UK) and HIPAA-aligned standards (US)


# 2. Core Insight: Data Is the Real Asset

The platform becomes the largest neutral intelligence layer sitting _above_
providers.

Providers see only:

- Their own patients
- Their own outcomes
- Their own pricing

The platform sees:

- Cross-provider performance
- Cross-treatment efficacy
- Longitudinal outcomes across the entire market

This creates leverage no single clinic can replicate.

# 3. Primary Data Monetisation Paths (Near-Term)

Provider Intelligence & Benchmarking (B2B SaaS)

Offer providers paid access to anonymised, aggregated insights, including:

- Treatment conversion rates vs market average
- Retention and churn benchmarks
- Price elasticity by demographic
- Biomarker improvement trends (aggregated)
- Drop-off points in treatment journeys

This is:

- Non-identifiable
- Non-diagnostic
- Extremely valuable to providers

Providers already pay for:

- Ads
- Guesswork
- Internal analytics that lack context

This replaces that spend.

2. Optimised Provider Performance Fees

As data depth increases, the platform can:

- Dynamically route users to higher-performing treatments
- Improve matching accuracy
- Increase lifetime value per user


This allows:

- Higher commission capture justified by higher conversion
- Performance-tiered commercial agreements

Importantly:

- Treatment ranking remains clinical-fit first
- Commercial upside is captured _after_ correct matching
3. Supplement & Non-Prescription Intelligence Layer

Anonymised data enables:

- Evidence-backed supplement stack optimisation
- Demand forecasting for specific compounds
- Outcome-linked supplement performance

This supports:

- Higher affiliate conversion
- Private-label product development (future)
- More defensible supplement recommendations

# 4. Secondary Data Monetisation (Mid-Term)

1.Life Sciences & Research Partnerships

Aggregated, anonymised datasets can be licensed to:

- Research institutions
- Life sciences companies
- Diagnostics companies
- Insurers (non-underwriting use only)

Use cases:

- Population-level biomarker trends
- Treatment adoption patterns
- Real-world evidence generation
- Hypothesis validation for new therapies

This is:

- Opt-in at user level
- High-margin
- Low operational burden
2. Diagnostics Optimisation & Lab Partnerships

The platform can monetise insights by:

- Designing better diagnostic panels
- Optimising biomarker bundles


- Forecasting demand by geography and cohort

Labs benefit from:

- Higher volume
- Better panel design
- Reduced churn

This strengthens lab-side economics and bargaining power.

# 5. What the Platform Will Not Do

To preserve trust and regulatory safety, the platform will not:

- Sell identifiable user data
- Sell raw medical records
- Allow advertising access to user profiles
- Allow treatment targeting outside user-initiated flows

Data monetisation is:

- Aggregated
- Anonymised
- Insight-based
- Value-aligned

- The intelligence backbone of non-surgical healthcare

This creates:
